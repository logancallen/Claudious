## 1. Texas school district sports flooring procurement — 2026 trends

**Price/cost environment:**
- School gym construction is running **$190–$320/sq ft** in 2026.
- Multi-sport complexes are **$210–$425+/sq ft**.
- Both numbers reflect rising material costs and sustained post-pandemic demand for athletic-facility upgrades. Implication for Courtside Pro: quote-sticker-shock is real for procurement officers — tooling that itemizes scope and shows like-for-like vs. industry bands adds credibility.

**Demand drivers:**
- Preventive wellness and injury-reduction emphasis is driving demand for shock-absorbing and antimicrobial surfaces — vinyl systems (Tarkett/Gerflor class) gaining share in schools over pure hardwood for secondary gyms, though MFMA maple remains dominant for primary competition courts.
- Multi-hue segmentation (basketball + volleyball + pickleball on same surface with contrasting markings) is being specified more frequently — pickleball inclusion is the notable 2026 shift.
- Custom branding (inlaid logos, district color palettes) is being marketed as a sponsorship/wayfinding feature, not a cosmetic upsell. Worth positioning in Courtside Pro quote templates as a standard line item, not an optional add-on.

**Real-world signal:**
- Katy ISD updated its schools' gym floors with new vinyl sports flooring, cited for lower maintenance cost and non-slip injury protection. Katy is a BuyBoard-heavy district — vinyl-over-hardwood is a live specification conversation with Texas procurement officers.

**Sustainability angle:**
- Districts are being pushed toward recycled-content and energy-efficient specifications (bond language, state-level sustainability goals). Vendors that can document recycled content and VOC certifications are at an advantage on RFPs.

## 4. Competitor landscape — sports flooring SaaS

**Headline finding: the "sports flooring SaaS" category does not exist as a named segment in 2026 market coverage.**

- Market research coverage of "sports flooring" focuses on the physical product market (valued ~$2.95B in 2025, projected $4.16B by 2035, 3.5% CAGR). The competitor names here are manufacturers — Regupol, Ecore International, MONDO Worldwide — and contractor/installers — Connor Sports, Action Sports Flooring, Beynon Sports.
- Flooring-business software exists as a broader category ("best flooring software 2026"), led by generalist products like **Service Buddy** covering CRM, digital quoting, inventory, job tracking, and payments for flooring dealers and installers. None of these are sports-flooring specific.
- Implication: Courtside Pro's competitive set is not a direct software competitor — it is **the generalist flooring-business SaaS tools** (Service Buddy-class) and **homegrown spreadsheets + QuickBooks** that most small-to-mid sports-flooring contractors actually use today. The wedge is domain specificity: MFMA spec language, BuyBoard-line-item pricing, district-procurement workflows, and the 2027 re-award cycle.

**Relevance to Courtside Pro positioning:**
- "No direct competitor" is both the opportunity and the risk. No competitor means no market education done for you — buyers have to be taught that a sports-flooring-specific tool is worth paying for vs. their current setup.
- The competitive differentiator story should lead with **Texas public-procurement depth** (BuyBoard, MFMA, TEA/district bond workflows), not generic "better than a spreadsheet."
- Worth monitoring: if a generalist like Service Buddy launches a sports-surfacing vertical, that's a signal to accelerate. No such move surfaced in this pass.
